Augmented Reality


Augmented Reality (AR) is an interactive experience of combining a real-world environment with enhancements provided by computer-generated (CG) perceptual information. Unlike VR, AR is not a completely immersive, virtual experience. Virtual objects or displays lay over the real world with positional tracking, typically through a set of smart glasses, goggles, a monocle, or an HMD .This technology is especially useful for military and medical purposes as both require much focus and information in high-stress, timely scenarios. VR

Virtual Reality


VR is a fully immersive experience composed entirely of computer-generated environments and objects or 360º videos. So, what this means is, the term "virtual reality" can only be applied to programs that completely change the environment around the user. To explain, Virtual Reality is created by using sensory stimulators to trick the user’s brain into believing they are in a separate reality. This can be accomplished by using headsets known as head-mounted displays (HMD).

MR

Mixed Reality


In the Simulation industry, Mixed Reality refers to what is also known as Augmented Virtuality (AV). It is the addition of tangible, real-world objects to a virtual environment using external cameras. However, MR is also known as a spectrum between VR and the real world. L&D Solution Mixed Reality is anywhere between the extrema of the virtual continuum. This virtual continuum includes AR and AV. With MR technology at our fingertips, L&D Simulation can develop scenarios, missions, and environments to integrate with real cockpits, controls, and devices to create effective immersion for training.
Mixed reality is the next wave in computing followed by mainframes, PCs, and smartphones and it seems to be going mainstream for consumers and businesses, thus liberating our screen-bound experiences by giving us instinctual interactions with data in our living spaces and with our friends. On top of that, thanks to our experience of working with other Indian defence establishments like the Navy, and Army just like you, we understand the ground reality standing in your way to those outcomes.
Based on the information that we have obtained so far; we’ve outlined our primary goals as below: • Training of troops in an environment of current weaponry. • Training of troops in newly procured weaponry. • High costs of training in terms of spent ammunition, wear and tear of weapons. • Constant need for refresher training of troops to keep them in a battle-ready format.

Lux and Decibels wish to create a world-class virtual reality-based training program for the Indian Defence establishment. The program will help train troops in simulated real war-like conditions in a virtual environment with a real feel of weapons and other dimensions via simulation. It’s been acknowledged that the cost of mock yet realistic combat training with real gear, weaponry, ammunition, and simulated conditions can run into a few lakhs or to even crores every time. Hence using our custom-designed training module will help impart the cutting-edge skills needed to react in a real battlefield scenario. We are confident that this independent training module with artificial intelligence will help bridge the gap in training and provide constant feedback matrix on battle preparedness.
